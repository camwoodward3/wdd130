Home page

Super Mario is a platform game series created by Nintendo on October 1985, starring their mascot, Mario. It is the central series of the greater Mario franchise. At least one Super Mario game has been released for every major Nintendo console. There are more than 20 games in the series. The games are set primarily in the Mushroom Kingdom, typically with Mario as the player character, along with his brother, Luigi and other members of the Mario cast. History

Super Mario Bros Released: October 1985 Platform: Nintendo Entertainment System (NES) Plot: Super Mario Bros is set in the fantasy land of the Mushroom Kingdom after Mario and Luigi had arrived through a clay pipe from New York City In the Mushroom Kingdom, a tribe of Koopa Troopas invade the kingdom and uses the magic of its king, Bowser, to turn its inhabitants, the Toads, into inanimate objects. They also kidnap Princess Peach, the princess of the Mushroom Kingdom and the only one with the ability to reverse Bowser's spell. After hearing the news, the brothers set out to save the princess and free the kingdom from Bowser. Super Mario Bros 2 Released: September 1988 Platform: Nintendo Entertainment System (NES) Plot: Mario has a dream of a staircase leading to a mysterious door to a mysterious place. A voice identifies the world as the dreamland of Subcon, and asks for Mario's help in defeating the villainous frog named Wart, a tyrant who has cursed Subcon and its people. Mario suddenly awakes and decides to tell Luigi, Toad, and Princess Peach, who all report experiencing the same dream. Super Mario Bros 3 Released: February 12, 1990 Platform: Nintendo Entertainment System (NES) Plot: The Mushroom World is invaded by the Koopalings, Bowser's seven children. The Koopalings conquer each of the seven kingdoms by stealing its king's magical wand and using it to transform him into an animal. Princess Peach sends Mario and Luigi to travel to each kingdom to retrieve the stolen wand, and restore the kings back to normal, and to save Princess Peach from Bowser. Super Mario World Released: August 23, 1991 Platform: Super Nintendo Entertainment System (SNES) Plot: Following the events in Super Mario Bros.Â 3, Mario and Luigi decide to go on vacation with Princess Peach to a place called Dinosaur Land. Mario and Luigi soon realize that Bowser's forces must have captured Peach as well, so Mario, Luigi and Yoshi set out to save Peach and Yoshi's dinosaur friends, traversing through Dinosaur Land for Bowser and his Koopalings. Super Mario 64 Released: September 30, 1996 Platform: Nintendo 64 Plot: The game begins with a letter from Princess Peach inviting Mario to come to her castle for a cake she has baked for him. When he arrives, Mario discovers that Bowser has invaded the castle and imprisoned the princess and her servants within its walls using the power of the castle's 120 Power Stars. The Power Stars are hidden in the castle's paintings, which serve as portals to other worlds where Bowser's minions keep watch over the Stars. Mario explores the castle and enters these worlds, gaining access to more rooms as he recovers more Stars. Super Mario Sunshine Released: August 26, 2002 Platform: GameCube Plot: The game takes place on Isle Delfino, a dolphin-shaped tropical island mainly inhabited by the Pianta and Noki people. Mario sets out for Isle Delfino for a vacation with Princess Peach, her steward Toadsworth, and a group of five Toads. Upon arrival at Delfino Airstrip, they discover a mass of paint-like goop. After acquiring the Flash Liquidizing Ultra Dousing Device (F.L.U.D.D.), a water cannon created by Professor E. Gadd, Mario defeats a slime-covered Piranha Plant that emerges from the goop. However, Mario is arrested on suspicion of vandalizing the island with graffiti, which has caused the source of the island's power, Shine Sprites, to disappear and the island to be covered in shadow. After being convicted, Mario is assigned community service to clean up the island and track down the real criminal behind this.
